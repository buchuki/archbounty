Introduction

Welcome to Arch Bounty. The simple goal of this site is to allow Arch Linux users to pay other users to complete Arch related tasks. Arch Bounty is comprised of Projects, Donations, Contributions, and Discussion.

This is not an official Arch Linux Project. I am a member of the Arch Linux development team, and I hope developers will use the project both to earn money for themselves on specific bounties and to solicit community participation on other tasks by offering donation funds. However, this is an unofficial side project I am contributing to the community.

Description

Projects are concrete Arch-related tasks that someone wishes to be completed. Anyone can create a project. You may create a project and donate to it if you want that task completed. You can also create a project and complete it in hopes that someone will donate to your work. Projects must be clearly described and well-defined so that it is easy for the community to tell when they have been completed, and who has done the work. Please, for my sake, keep projects small and managable, at least at first. Projects must be completable by anyone, and must not require specific permissions. For example, you can not create a project to have a developer update a package more quickly. You can, however, create a project to request a new PKGBUILD, as anyone can create and post a PKGBUILD. It will not be possible to donate to a project until a moderator has approved that it is well-defined and acceptable.

Donations are specific monetary values assigned to a project from a single user. Anyone can make a donation. Donations are processed through AlertPay. One project can have multiple donations from multiple users.

Donations can have an expiry date. This is useful if you want a project to be completed before a specific date. When a donation expires, funds are transferred to the Arch Linux Project. You will not get your money back if a project you donated to is not completed on time. Think of Arch Bounty as a way to influence what your donation contributes to, not as a command or demand. If the donation does not have an expiry, it will remain attached to the project until it is completed or cancelled.

Contributions are pieces of work to help complete a project. Anyone can make a contribution. When a project is deemed complete, all non-expired donations to that project will be distributed to contributors through AlertPay.

When you make a contribution, you must describe in detail how you have contributed to that project. Links to closed bug reports, git or subversion patches, forum or mailing list discussions, or external project pages demonstrating exactly what you have done will help.

When you make a contribution you make an estimate as to what percentage of the project your contribution has completed. I would appreciate it if at first you complete entire projects in one contribution to keep things simple. Longer term, multiple contributors will need to be able to cooperate on larger projects. Donations will be split between the contributions weighted by this percentage.

Discussions are attached to all projects and all contributions. Discussions can be used when projects are first posted to request clarification or modification of the requirements. They can be used to report progress while completing the project. On contributions, discussions can discuss whether a person has over- or under-valued their contribution to the whole.

Moderation

I am hoping that the community will be largely self-moderating and will cooperatively decide when and where funds should be distributed. In the beginning, I will personally oversee the process and try to settle disputes. I will release funds to contributors only after I have decided the project is truly completed and have determined who has made contributions.

I anticipate two possible kinds of disputes. The first kind is people claiming to have completed the project while donors believe there are still issues outstanding. The second kind is people claiming they have contributed more than their fair share to a project when other contributors want their percentage.

The first dispute can be avoided by being very particular about the requirements when creating a project. Projects will be reviewed and donations will not be accepted until the requirements are fully specified.

The second dispute cannot be mitigated by active discussion of who is doing what and ensuring that people are working together, cooperatively, instead of competing.

If the process seems to be working, I will invite trusted community members to join the moderation team and help me decide when project requirements are specific enough, when they have been completed, and how to distribute donations among contributors.

If the process results in arguments, flamewars, and less productivity rather than more, I will simply cancel the service and deem it a failure.

Disclaimer

This is a highly experimental endeavour. The Arch Linux community is known for behaving sensibly and cooperatively. Introducing money into the mix could destroy that, as greed tends to do. If that happens, the project will be cancelled.

This project is meant to help encourage timely completion of jobs through the use of funding. It does not guarantee that a project will be completed before a specific date or at all. It does not promise that contributors will receive any or fair remuneration for their efforts.

All donations to a project are donations only. You are not making a purchase and do not have the right to demand, command, or control the development process or completion. Assume your donations are donations to the Arch Linux project, but you can suggest where they be directed by applying to a specific project.

All contributions to a project are contributions only. You are not making a contract and do not have the right to demand payment in part or in full. Assume your contributions are unpaid open source contributions to the Arch Linux community, but you may receive some monetary compensation.

I control all funds and could, in theory, run off with them at any time. If you do not trust me, please don't use the service. As many users have entrusted me with Arch Schwag and I have been contributing to this community for many years, I do not anticipate this being a problem.
